**力と美のために** — *for the sake of power and beauty.*

Ryoku is a daily-driver Linux for the half of cybersecurity life that doesn't fit in a throwaway VM: studying, lab work, CTFs, courseware, and tinkering on your own equipment. Steam, Discord, your IDE, your TryHackMe lab, the OpenVPN to your home router — on one machine that you actually live in.

The desktop is [Niri](https://github.com/YaLTeR/niri), a scrollable-tiling Wayland compositor, with a [Quickshell](https://quickshell.outfoxxed.me)-based topbar, sidebars, lock screen, and settings panel. Workflow primitives that matter to security work are built into the shell instead of bolted on as separate tray icons.

## What ships

- **Desktop**: Niri tiling session, Quickshell topbar / sidebars / lock / launcher / settings, SDDM with switchable themes.
- **Security primitives**: in-shell OpenVPN profile manager, Tailscale + OpenVPN bar indicators (SecPulse), hardened defaults (UFW, encrypted home, no telemetry phoning home).
- **Daily-driver fit**: Steam, Discord, dev toolchains (mise, Docker, common languages), media stack (mpv, browsers, image / video tools).
- **Brand**: cohesive Greek-Noir palette, switchable Material 3 skins (`material`, `aurora`, `angel`, `ryoku-shell`, `cards`).

## Scope

Ryoku is built for **learning and personal practice**: TryHackMe, Hack The Box, OffSec courseware, CTFs, your own home lab, your own router, and any system you own or have explicit written authorization to test.

Ryoku is **not** a Kali or Parrot replacement for real engagement work. Professional pentesting needs client-isolated environments, clean attribution, and snapshot-and-revert disposability that a daily-driver OS deliberately doesn't give you. Use a dedicated VM, or better a dedicated engagement laptop, for that.

You are responsible for how you use what ships here. Unauthorized scanning, exploitation, or interference with systems you do not own is illegal in most jurisdictions.
